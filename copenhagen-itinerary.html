🚶 Strøget + Round Tower (Rundetårn)
Walk from Rådhuspladsen along Strøget toward the city. About halfway, turn off to visit the Round Tower — built 1637–1642, it's Copenhagen's oldest functioning observatory. What makes it special: no stairs, only a wide spiral equestrian ramp all the way to the top (~35m). Very gentle on the legs — ideal for mum. Views over the old city rooftops are excellent. Back on Strøget, continue to Kongens Nytorv. ~1.5–2 hours total.
⚠️ 2026 restoration notice: The observatory dome and telescope are closed for restoration throughout 2026. The viewing platform at the top remains open — you still get the full panoramic views. Also note: the Round Tower is NOT included in the Copenhagen Card (removed April 2024). Buy tickets on the door — no advance purchase available.

💡 Senior discount note: Copenhagen's standard senior discount applies from age 65. Your parents are 60, so they pay the adult rate of DKK 60/person here. No student/senior tier applies yet — this is the case at most Copenhagen attractions.

⛪ Christianshavn + Church of Our Saviour (Vor Frelsers Kirke)
Cross to charming Christianshavn and head straight to the Church of Our Saviour — a magnificent Baroque church built in 1752, with one of Copenhagen's most beautiful interiors: a grand marble altarpiece, intricate carved pews, and a monumental pipe organ. For Catholic visitors the church itself is deeply worth seeing at ground level. The famous exterior spiral staircase winds 400 steps around the golden spire — the final 150 or so are outside on the tower exterior, exposed to wind and weather. Take it at your own pace — there are rest points on the way up, and the views at the top are spectacular. ~1.5 hours.

🧭 Practical Tips & Reminders

📍 Your Commute (ØK → CitizenM)

Walk ~12 min to DR Byen (M1) → 3 stops to København H → 8 min walk to CitizenM. Total: ~25–30 min. For later evenings, same route back. Set a Google Maps favourite for DR Byen to make navigation easy.

👴 Senior Discounts — What to Know

Copenhagen's standard senior rate applies from age 65. At 60, your parents pay adult prices at most attractions. That said: DSB trains offer senior discounts at 65+, and the Royal Theatre offers half-price tickets at 65+. The Copenhagen Card is the most effective way to save. Always worth asking "er der rabat for seniorer?" (is there a senior discount?) at each venue.

🦶 Mum-Friendly Access Notes

Flat/good: Strøget, Nyhavn (use inner path), King's Garden, Kastellet, Assistens Cemetery, Christiansborg (tower has lift ✓), Round Tower (ramp only, no stairs ✓). Manageable with breaks: Rosenborg Castle (stairs, no lift — ground floor + jewels are highlights), Church of Our Saviour tower (400 steps, rest points throughout). Cobblestones: Nyhavn quayside, old city. Recommend cushioned flat shoes with grip.

🎫 Copenhagen Card — Is It Worth It?

A 72-hr card costs ~DKK 779/person. It covers: Rosenborg (DKK 140) + Home of Carlsberg (DKK 215) + canal tour (DKK 120) + all metro/bus for 3 days (~DKK 200 value) = ~DKK 675 in paid attractions alone. At 3 people the card pays for itself. Note: Round Tower NOT included (removed April 2024). Get at copenhagencard.com.

🎨 Free Sunday — Ny Carlsberg Glyptotek

Day 5 is Sunday — and the Glyptotek (world-class art museum with Egyptian mummies, Rodin sculptures, Impressionist paintings) is FREE every Sunday. It's 5 min walk from CitizenM and right next to the National Museum. Fits perfectly into the Day 5 afternoon as an optional extra — or replace the National Museum with it if preferred.

📱 Useful Apps

DSB (train tickets), Rejseplanen (route planning), Rejsekort (transit card), Google Maps for walking routes around Copenhagen.

🚶 Getting Around

Copenhagen is very flat and walkable. Most attractions near CitizenM (Rådhuspladsen) are under 20 min on foot. M3 ring line is the most useful for parents — it's a simple loop. From Day 3 onwards they can navigate independently. Rejseplanen app handles all routing.

💰 Money

Denmark uses Danish Krone (DKK). ~DKK 7 ≈ €1. Card payments accepted almost everywhere — cash is rarely needed. Dining out is expensive; lunch menus are better value than dinners.

🧱 LEGO House — Book Ahead

Sells out, especially on Fridays. Buy at legohouse.com as soon as your trip is confirmed. Also pre-book the Mini Chef restaurant and DSB train tickets.

🌶️ Poulette Tips

Very casual — expect to eat standing or on a bench. Goes mild if asked. No sit-down restaurant. Go early as it sells out, especially on weekends.

🌧️ March Weather

~5–9°C, rain likely. Pack waterproof layers. Canal boat tours are covered/heated. Most attractions are indoors. Sunset is around 6:15pm — plan outdoor sightseeing for the afternoon.
