🚶 Strøget + Round Tower (Rundetårn)
Walk from Rådhuspladsen along Strøget toward the city. About halfway, turn off to visit the Round Tower — built 1637–1642, it's Copenhagen's oldest functioning observatory. What makes it special: no stairs, only a wide spiral equestrian ramp all the way to the top (~35m). Very gentle on the legs — ideal for mum. Views over the old city rooftops are excellent. Back on Strøget, continue to Kongens Nytorv. ~1.5–2 hours total.
⚠️ 2026 restoration notice: The observatory dome and telescope are closed for restoration throughout 2026. The viewing platform at the top remains open — you still get the full panoramic views. Buy tickets on the door — no advance purchase available.

🌈 Nyhavn — Colorful Canal District
The most iconic spot in Copenhagen. Stroll the 300m waterfront, admire the pastel townhouses, spot the tall ships. Hans Christian Andersen's former homes are at no. 18, 20, and 67. Plenty of outdoor seats and benches along the water. Short and manageable for mum — only ~300m end-to-end. ~45 min.

🌿 King's Garden (Kongens Have) — Morning Walk
Denmark's oldest royal garden, dating to 1606. Peaceful, flat, and beautiful — ideal for mum. Gravel and paved paths throughout, plenty of benches. A short 15–20 min stroll before the castle opens. In March, early spring flowers may be starting to bloom. Flat and bench-friendly throughout. ~20 min.

🏰 Rosenborg Castle — Crown Jewels & Royal Treasures
One of Copenhagen's finest attractions. Built by King Christian IV in 1606 — 24 royal rooms across 3 floors, plus the spectacular Crown Jewels in the basement treasury. Self-guided with leaflets in multiple languages. ~1h45min — plan to start heading out at 11:45am to catch the Guard march.
♿ The castle has multiple floors connected by stairs — no lift. Mum can still see the ground floor rooms and the Crown Jewels (basement level) which are the highlights. Suggest taking it slow and using the castle garden café as a rest point midway.

The Royal Guard marches from Rosenborg every day at ~11:30am through the city streets, arriving at Amalienborg Palace at exactly noon. You can watch the full parade by starting at Rosenborg at 11:45am and walking along the route, or simply arrive at Amalienborg by 11:50am for a good spot. Free. ~30 min.

⛺ Kastellet — Copenhagen's Star-Shaped Fortress
A lovely 15-minute walk north along Langelinie Promenade from Amalienborg. Kastellet is one of the best-preserved Renaissance star-shaped fortresses in Northern Europe — built in 1626 and still an active military base today. Walk the flat grass ramparts above the moat canals, spot the old windmill and the small garrison church, and watch locals walking dogs and reading on the lawns. A genuine hidden gem: peaceful, photogenic, and historically rich without a single queue. Flat throughout — ideal for mum. Excellent benches along the ramparts. ~45 min.
Fortress · History💡 Free · Flat grass ramparts · Benches throughout ✓ · Open daily
3:00 PM
⛪ Christianshavn + Church of Our Saviour (Vor Frelsers Kirke)
Cross to charming Christianshavn and head straight to the Church of Our Saviour — a magnificent Baroque church built in 1752, with one of Copenhagen's most beautiful interiors: a grand marble altarpiece, intricate carved pews, and a monumental pipe organ. For Catholic visitors the church itself is deeply worth seeing at ground level. The famous exterior spiral staircase winds 400 steps around the golden spire — the final 150 or so are outside on the tower exterior, exposed to wind and weather. Take it at your own pace — there are rest points on the way up, and the views at the top are spectacular. ~1.5 hours.
Baroque Church · Tower💡 Church interior: FREE · Tower: DKK 50 · 400 steps, rest points en route · Not suitable in heavy wind/rain

🏺 National Museum (Nationalmuseet) — Viking Age to Modern Denmark
One of the finest free museums in Scandinavia, and perfectly timed for a quiet Saturday morning before the crowds arrive. Covers 10,000 years of Danish history: the Sun Chariot (one of the world's great Bronze Age objects), Viking Age swords and runestones, Ice Age finds, Egyptian mummies, and a whole wing on Danish folk culture. Plenty of benches and seating throughout — take it at a genuinely relaxed pace, sit and read the panels, linger in the rooms you love. No closing-time pressure at all. FREE always. ~3 hours.

🌶️ Lunch — Poulette (Møllegade 1, Nørrebro)
Copenhagen's cult fried chicken sandwich spot — featured in The Bear, beloved by the city's chefs. The signature Nashville spicy fried chicken or mapo tofu sandwich on brioche. Add seasoned fries. Tiny takeaway spot with no indoor seating — once you have your order, walk 2 min to Assistens Cemetery or Blågårds Plads for plenty of benches. ~40 min.

🌿 Frederiksberg Gardens or 🏭 Home of Carlsberg (Choose One)
Option A — Frederiksberg Gardens (recommended): One of Copenhagen's most beautiful royal gardens — a sweeping Baroque landscape surrounding Frederiksberg Palace, with long tree-lined avenues, a serene canal system, a Chinese pavilion, and resident herons along the water. Completely flat, free to enter, and open until sunset. Ideal for a relaxed 1.5-hour stroll with plenty of benches and zero crowds on a Saturday afternoon. A 10-minute walk from the Meatpacking District for dinner. Free. ~1.5 hours.

Option B — Home of Carlsberg: The original 1847 brewery with a 3,000m² exhibition — bottle collection, draught horses, elephant gateway, beer tasting included. Note: closes at 5pm, meaning only ~30 min of visit time after transit from Nørrebro — not recommended on a Pompette day. If your parents are keen on Carlsberg, the best approach is to skip Pompette that day so you can arrive at Carlsberg by ~3:30pm and get a proper 1.5 hours. DKK 215/person — pre-book at homeofcarlsberg.com.

🎨 Two Museum Days — Plan Ahead

Day 4 (Saturday): National Museum in the morning (10am–1pm). Always FREE. Quieter early on — aim for the Viking and Bronze Age wings first; the Sun Chariot alone is worth the trip. Day 5 (Sunday): Ny Carlsberg Glyptotek, FREE on Sundays only. Full afternoon from 1:15pm — enough time to explore properly and linger in the winter garden café. Both museums are within 10–15 minutes of CitizenM on foot.

🚶 Getting Around

Copenhagen is very flat and walkable. Most attractions near CitizenM (Rådhuspladsen) are under 20 min on foot. M3 ring line is the most useful for parents — it's a simple loop. From Day 4 onwards they navigate independently. Rejseplanen app handles all routing.

💰 Money

Denmark uses Danish Krone (DKK). ~DKK 7 ≈ €1. Card payments accepted almost everywhere — cash is rarely needed. Dining out is expensive; lunch menus are better value than dinners.

🧱 LEGO House — All Booked ✅

🌶️ Poulette + 🍷 Pompette — The Møllegade Combo

Poulette is very casual — expect to eat on a bench nearby. Goes mild if asked. No sit-down, no reservations. Go by 1:30pm on Saturdays as it sells out. Pompette is literally next door (Møllegade 3) — a relaxed natural wine bar, great for dad to browse small-producer European bottles with knowledgeable staff. No booking needed; walk in and take a seat at the bar or a table.
